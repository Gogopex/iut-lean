Inter-universal Teichmuller theory

IUT as a map of alien arithmetic worlds.

Inter-universal Teichmuller theory is Mochizuki's proposed arithmetic deformation theory for number fields equipped with elliptic-curve data. It replaces direct comparison of ring/scheme structures by a network of Hodge theaters, theta links, log links, indeterminacies, and log-volume estimates.

Enter the wiki Open source library Lean efforts
Global route source objects to Diophantine bounds

Orientation

IUT is not best approached as a single calculation. It is a theory of controlled comparison between arithmetic structures that are deliberately not kept inside one global ring or scheme history. The vocabulary is unusual because the comparison method is unusual.

Objects

The starting objects are initial theta data, hyperbolic orbicurves, Frobenioid-type structures, log-shells, and Hodge theaters.

Motion

The central motion is through theta links and log links, arranged in the log-theta lattice. These links compare weakened data rather than ordinary scheme identities.

Output

The proof-critical output is a log-volume comparison in IUT III, Corollary 3.12. IUT IV then applies this comparison to Diophantine inequalities.

Wiki convention. This page explains the source theory and its formalization interfaces. It does not assert that the disputed proof is settled. When a statement is a conditional interface, the page says so.

Initial Theta Data

Initial theta data is the input package for the four-paper series. In IUT I, it includes an elliptic curve over a number field, a prime l >= 5, selected places, torsion data, and technical hypotheses that allow Hodge theaters and theta-link structures to be built.

Why it matters

Initial theta data fixes the arithmetic environment before the theory starts moving between copies. Later comparisons must remember which objects are input data, which are transported, and which have had their ring/scheme history weakened.

Formalization role

This is one of the first places where a Lean development must choose between a faithful construction and a typed record interface. Dupuy-Hilado show how parts of the initial theta data can be made explicit for the curve 11a1.

Sources: IUT I Dupuy-Hilado initial theta data Related: Hodge theaters Corollary 3.12

Hodge Theaters

A Hodge theater is a miniature arithmetic universe built from the initial data. Mochizuki describes these theaters as models in which the additive and multiplicative dimensions of a number field are disentangled enough to support comparisons unavailable in ordinary scheme theory.

Separated dimensions one theater keeps linked data typed, not globally identified

The phrase "alien arithmetic holomorphic structure" refers to viewing data from the standpoint of a theater related by a non-ring/scheme-theoretic link. This is why the theory has to track histories, labels, and comparison levels explicitly.

Sources: IUT I Panoramic Overview Related: theta/log links dispute

Frobenioids and Log-Shells

Frobenioids and log-shells are part of the infrastructure that lets IUT talk about arithmetic line bundles, monoids, units, value groups, and logarithmic structures without immediately returning to ordinary schemes.

Frobenioid direction

Frobenioid language packages divisor-like and line-bundle-like data in a categorical form. In the IUT route, this supports multiplicative/arithmetic bookkeeping that later participates in log-volume estimates.

Log-shell direction

Log-shells arise from local logarithms and make it possible to discuss additive logarithmic data associated to local units. They are essential for the vertical direction of the log-theta lattice.

A formal wiki should not treat these as decorative names. They are the layer where the theory prepares the exact kind of weakened, typed information that later comparisons are allowed to see.
Sources: IUT I IUT III Related: log-theta lattice multiradiality

Theta Links and Log Links

The two principal directions of the log-theta lattice are different in kind. Theta links come from Hodge-Arakelov-theoretic evaluation of theta values; log links come from applying local logarithms and passing through log-shells.

LinkSourceFunction in the theoryRisk if misread
Theta link IUT II Relates Hodge theaters via theta-value data at torsion points. Reading it as an ordinary ring/scheme morphism remembers too much.
Log link IUT III Uses local logarithms to pass into log-shell data. Forgetting calibration can destroy the intended comparison meaning.
Sources: IUT II IUT III Related: log-theta lattice

Log-Theta Lattice

The log-theta lattice is the two-dimensional noncommutative diagram of Hodge theaters studied in IUT III. Horizontal motion is theta-link motion; vertical motion is log-link motion. The noncommutativity is not incidental: it is a source of the comparison constraints and indeterminacies.

Log-theta lattice horizontal theta links, vertical log links

Indeterminacies

Indeterminacies are controlled ambiguities that arise because IUT compares weakened data across non-scheme-theoretic links. In the Theorem 3.11 to Corollary 3.12 corridor, the important distinction is between invariance under the first two indeterminacies and a one-sided upper-semi relation for the third.

Ind1

Procession-type symmetry. In the current Lean route, it is modeled by preservation of the relevant normalized log-volume expression.

Ind2

Local tensor or symmetry-type ambiguity. The corridor again treats the relevant normalized quantity as invariant.

Ind3

Upper-semi ambiguity. This is not modeled as equality in the source-facing route; it supplies one-sided containment or inequality data.

The most dangerous simplification is to turn one-sided upper-semi data into equality of possible-image regions without proving that this stronger relation is licensed by the source construction.
Sources: IUT III Dupuy-Hilado probabilistic Szpiro Related: Corollary 3.12 dispute

Multiradiality, SHE, IPL, APT

Multiradial algorithms are meant to make sense from the viewpoint of an alien arithmetic holomorphic structure. The proof-critical corridor also uses named properties such as simultaneous holomorphic expressibility (SHE), input prime-strip linkage (IPL), and algorithmic parallel transport (APT).

Corollary 3.12 Corridor

The proof-critical corridor is the route from IUT III, Theorem 3.11 through Step (x), Step (xi), and the final signed real inequality. The core issue is how qualitative multiradial/common-container data becomes a numerical log-volume comparison without illicitly identifying histories or comparison levels.

Theorem 3.11 to Corollary 3.12 conditional interfaces made explicit

What is formal algebra

Once Lean has q_signed <= theta_signed, 0 < -q_signed, and the displayed theta_signed <= C_Theta * (-q_signed), the lower bound -1 <= C_Theta is elementary ordered-real algebra.

What remains mathematical source work

The hard part is constructing the bridge from IUT source data to the signed-log upper-ray membership of the q-pilot, while preserving the distinctions between labels, histories, real-line copies, and hull/log-volume levels.

IUT IV and Consequences

IUT IV applies the log-volume estimates obtained from the earlier papers to Diophantine inequalities, including Vojta-type, Szpiro-type, and abc-type consequences. Later work on explicit estimates modifies parts of the theory to obtain numerical effective constants.

Log-volume computation

IUT IV is where the abstract comparison machinery is converted into inequalities involving heights and conductors.

Set-theoretic foundations

The paper also discusses foundational issues around moving between copies, universes, and set-theoretic implementations.

Effective estimates

The 2022 explicit estimates paper gives numerically effective versions using a modified theory of etale theta functions.

Sources: IUT IV Explicit Estimates WRAP version

Scholze-Stix and Responses

The Scholze-Stix note focuses on the proof-critical comparison near IUT III, Corollary 3.12. The wiki treats this as an important page, not as the main theme of IUT. The theory itself remains the primary object.

Scholze-Stix objection

Their simplified model argues that the comparison needed for the abc application cannot be obtained as claimed; in their reading, the attempted passage loses the numerical content required for the inequality.

critiqueCorollary 3.12

Mochizuki-side response

Mochizuki and Hoshi maintain that the simplification remembers or identifies structures in a way that violates the intended IUT boundary: Hodge-theater histories are weakened before comparison, and this is essential.

responseHodge theaters
Formalization reframes the dispute into exact interfaces: what source data is present, which histories are erased, which transports are allowed, and which numerical bridge is actually proved.

RIMS Lean Effort

Mochizuki's April 2026 progress report frames Lean formalization partly as a communication tool. It proposes a staged path toward a LeanForm of IUT, with early attention on the logical structure around the route from Theorem 3.11, through a reorganized "3.11.5", to Corollary 3.12.

Formalization target source route decomposed into Lean-facing obligations

For this repository, the practical lesson is to keep every bridge explicit: if a common container is supplied, Lean should still ask what numerical chart, transport, calibration, or bound converts it into the final signed inequality.

Sources: Mochizuki 2026 Lean report Essential Logical Structure Related: LANA Corollary 3.12

LANA

LANA stands for Lean for ANAbelian geometry. ZEN Mathematics Center announced it publicly on 31 March 2026 as an international project involving ZEN University, Utrecht University, and the University of Alberta. Its first aim is Lean formalization of anabelian geometry; its second aim is formal verification of IUT.

Core members reported by ZEN

Fumiharu Kato, Johan Commelin, Kiran Kedlaya, Yuichiro Hoshi, and Adam Topaz, with additional junior participants.

Status as of public announcement

ZEN reports work since autumn 2023, a neutral stance, substantial clarity about understood and unresolved points, and an interim progress report planned for 17 July 2026.

LANA is independent from the RIMS Lean effort, but public materials indicate dialogue with Mochizuki and a focus on making points of contention precise enough for Lean.
Online sources: ZEN announcement Kato/ZMC remarks Related: RIMS Lean effort

Glossary

Short definitions for navigation. Each entry is intentionally compact; follow the internal links for context and source references.

Alien copy
A typed copy of arithmetic data whose history is not identified with the original ring/scheme history.
Anabelian geometry
Reconstruction of arithmetic/geometric objects from fundamental-group-like data; a major prerequisite background for IUT.
APT
Algorithmic parallel transport through IUT gluings, up to specified indeterminacy.
Corollary 3.12
The proof-critical IUT III comparison that supplies the signed log-volume inequality used downstream.
Frobenioid
Categorical packaging of arithmetic line-bundle/divisor/monoid data used in Mochizuki's earlier infrastructure.
Hodge theater
A miniature arithmetic world associated to initial theta data, designed to separate additive and multiplicative structures.
Indeterminacy
Controlled ambiguity allowed by the IUT comparison; in the key corridor, Ind3 is one-sided upper-semi rather than equality.
IPL
Input prime-strip link property tying output possibilities to input prime-strip data at the comparison boundary.
LANA
Lean for ANAbelian geometry, a ZEN Mathematics Center project to formalize anabelian geometry and verify IUT.
Log link
Vertical link in the log-theta lattice, formed via local logarithms and log-shells.
Log-shell
Local logarithmic object associated to units; used to manage additive log data.
Log-theta lattice
Two-dimensional diagram of Hodge theaters whose horizontal arrows are theta links and vertical arrows are log links.
Multiradial
Meaningful from multiple radial or alien holomorphic viewpoints, rather than tied to one ordinary scheme history.
Prime strip
A local/global strip of prime-indexed data used in the IUT comparison machinery.
SHE
Simultaneous holomorphic expressibility, a condition needed to express output data relative to the target holomorphic structure.
Theta link
Horizontal link between Hodge theaters produced from Hodge-Arakelov theta evaluation.
